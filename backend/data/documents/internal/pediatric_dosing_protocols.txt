DOCUMENT: SafeRx Pediatric Dosing Protocols
Type: Clinical Reference Document
Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Last Updated: December 1, 2023
Department: Pediatric Pharmacy Services

============================================================

PURPOSE
-------

This document provides standardized pediatric dosing guidelines for commonly prescribed medications. All doses are general guidelines; individual patient factors must always be considered.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES
------------------

1. Weight-Based Dosing: Most pediatric doses are calculated per kilogram (mg/kg)
2. Maximum Doses: Never exceed adult maximum doses regardless of weight
3. Age Considerations: Some medications have age-specific restrictions
4. Renal/Hepatic Function: Adjust doses for organ dysfunction
5. Double-Check Calculations: All pediatric doses require independent verification

AGE CLASSIFICATIONS
-------------------

- Neonate: 0-28 days
- Infant: 1-12 months
- Child: 1-12 years
- Adolescent: 12-18 years

PAIN AND FEVER MEDICATIONS
--------------------------

Acetaminophen (Tylenol):
- Dose: 10-15 mg/kg/dose every 4-6 hours
- Maximum single dose: 15 mg/kg (up to 1000 mg)
- Maximum daily dose: 75 mg/kg/day (up to 4000 mg/day)
- Available forms: Liquid 160mg/5mL, chewables, tablets
- Note: Use caution in hepatic impairment

Ibuprofen (Motrin, Advil):
- Dose: 5-10 mg/kg/dose every 6-8 hours
- Maximum single dose: 400 mg
- Maximum daily dose: 40 mg/kg/day (up to 1200 mg/day)
- Age restriction: Not recommended under 6 months
- Contraindications: Renal impairment, active bleeding, aspirin allergy

Naproxen (Aleve):
- Dose: 5-7 mg/kg/dose every 8-12 hours
- Maximum daily dose: 20 mg/kg/day (up to 1000 mg/day)
- Age restriction: Not recommended under 2 years
- Note: Longer half-life than ibuprofen

ANTIBIOTICS
-----------

Amoxicillin:
- Standard infections: 25-50 mg/kg/day divided every 8-12 hours
- Otitis media (high dose): 80-90 mg/kg/day divided every 12 hours
- Maximum dose: 3000 mg/day
- Duration: Typically 7-10 days (5 days for uncomplicated otitis)

Azithromycin (Zithromax):
- Community-acquired pneumonia: 10 mg/kg day 1, then 5 mg/kg days 2-5
- Otitis media: 10 mg/kg/day x 3 days OR 30 mg/kg single dose
- Maximum dose: 500 mg/day
- Available forms: Liquid 200mg/5mL, tablets

Amoxicillin-Clavulanate (Augmentin):
- Standard: 25-45 mg/kg/day (amoxicillin component) divided every 12 hours
- Severe infections: 80-90 mg/kg/day divided every 12 hours
- Use ES formulation (600mg/5mL) for high-dose therapy
- Maximum: 3000 mg amoxicillin/day

Ciprofloxacin:
- Dose: 10-20 mg/kg/dose every 12 hours
- Maximum dose: 750 mg/dose, 1500 mg/day
- AGE RESTRICTION: Generally avoided in children <18 years due to cartilage concerns
- Use only when no safer alternatives; document justification

RESPIRATORY MEDICATIONS
-----------------------

Albuterol (inhaled):
- Nebulizer: 0.15 mg/kg/dose (minimum 2.5 mg) every 4-6 hours PRN
- MDI with spacer: 2-4 puffs every 4-6 hours PRN
- Acute exacerbation: May give every 20 minutes x 3 doses

Prednisolone/Prednisone:
- Asthma exacerbation: 1-2 mg/kg/day (max 60 mg) for 3-5 days
- Croup: 2 mg/kg single dose (max 60 mg)
- Taper not required if duration <7 days

GASTROINTESTINAL MEDICATIONS
----------------------------

Omeprazole:
- Dose: 0.7-1 mg/kg/dose once daily
- Infants (1-11 months): 0.5-1 mg/kg/dose
- Maximum dose: 20 mg/day (children), 40 mg/day (adolescents)
- Note: Capsule contents may be sprinkled on applesauce

Ondansetron (Zofran):
- Dose: 0.15 mg/kg/dose every 8 hours (IV/PO)
- Maximum single dose: 8 mg
- ODT formulation useful for vomiting children
- QT prolongation risk at high doses

NEUROLOGICAL/PSYCHIATRIC
------------------------

Gabapentin:
- Neuropathic pain: Start 5 mg/kg/day, titrate to 10-35 mg/kg/day divided TID
- Maximum: Based on age and indication (typically 3600 mg/day in adolescents)
- Not FDA-approved <3 years for epilepsy

Sertraline:
- Depression/Anxiety: Start 25 mg/day, may increase to 50-200 mg/day
- Age restriction: FDA-approved for OCD in children ≥6 years
- Black box warning: Monitor for suicidal ideation in pediatric patients

Fluoxetine (Prozac):
- Depression: Start 10 mg/day, may increase to 20-60 mg/day
- Age restriction: FDA-approved for depression ≥8 years, OCD ≥7 years
- Only SSRI FDA-approved for pediatric depression

CARDIOVASCULAR
--------------

Lisinopril:
- Hypertension: Start 0.07 mg/kg/day (max initial 5 mg), titrate to 0.1-0.6 mg/kg/day
- Maximum: 40 mg/day
- Age restriction: ≥6 years for hypertension
- Monitor: Potassium, renal function

Metoprolol:
- Hypertension: 1-2 mg/kg/day divided BID (max 6 mg/kg/day or 200 mg/day)
- Available: Immediate release and extended release formulations
- Caution: Asthma, diabetes (masks hypoglycemia)

MEDICATIONS TO AVOID IN PEDIATRICS
----------------------------------

1. Aspirin: Risk of Reye syndrome in viral illness (children <18)
2. Codeine: FDA contraindicated in children <12 years
3. Tramadol: FDA contraindicated in children <12 years
4. Tetracyclines: Avoid <8 years (tooth staining, bone effects)
5. Fluoroquinolones: Use with caution (cartilage concerns)
6. Loperamide: Avoid <2 years
7. Bismuth subsalicylate: Avoid <12 years (salicylate content)

HIGH-ALERT MEDICATIONS
----------------------

The following require independent double-check before dispensing:
- Insulin
- Opioids
- Chemotherapy
- Anticoagulants
- Concentrated electrolytes

CALCULATION VERIFICATION
------------------------

All pediatric doses must be verified using:
1. Current weight (kg) - weighed within past week for inpatients
2. Age-appropriate dosing references
3. Independent calculation by second healthcare professional
4. Maximum dose check against adult limits
