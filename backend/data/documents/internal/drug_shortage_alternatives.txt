PURPOSE
-------

This document provides therapeutic alternatives for medications currently in shortage or at risk of shortage. All substitutions should be evaluated for patient-specific factors.

CURRENT SHORTAGE STATUS LEVELS
------------------------------

Level 1 (Green): Available, no supply concerns
Level 2 (Yellow): Limited supply, monitor usage
Level 3 (Orange): Critical shortage, restrict to essential use
Level 4 (Red): Unavailable, use alternatives only

============================================================

CARDIOVASCULAR MEDICATIONS
--------------------------

LISINOPRIL TABLETS (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 10mg and 20mg tablets limited
Therapeutic alternatives:
1. Enalapril 10-20mg daily (equivalent to lisinopril 10-20mg)
2. Ramipril 5-10mg daily (more potent, adjust dose)
3. Benazepril 20-40mg daily
Notes: All ACE inhibitors have similar efficacy; monitor potassium and creatinine with any switch

METOPROLOL TARTRATE (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

AMLODIPINE (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

WARFARIN (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 5mg tablets intermittently unavailable
Alternatives:
1. Combine available strengths (1mg + 2mg tablets)
2. Consider DOAC switch if appropriate (see anticoagulation protocol):
   - Apixaban (Eliquis): For non-valvular AFib, VTE
   - Rivaroxaban (Xarelto): For non-valvular AFib, VTE
3. NOT interchangeable: Mechanical heart valves must remain on warfarin
Notes: DOAC switch requires renal function assessment and insurance verification

ATORVASTATIN (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

============================================================

DIABETES MEDICATIONS
--------------------

METFORMIN IR TABLETS (Status: Level 3 - Orange)
Shortage details: 500mg tablets in critical shortage
Alternatives:
1. Metformin ER 500mg (take once or twice daily instead of TID)
2. Metformin liquid 500mg/5mL (limited supply)
3. Split 1000mg tablets (if scored)
Notes: ER formulation may have different GI tolerability; monitor blood glucose during transition

GLIPIZIDE (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

============================================================

ANTIBIOTICS
-----------

AMOXICILLIN SUSPENSION (Status: Level 3 - Orange)
Shortage details: 250mg/5mL and 400mg/5mL limited
Alternatives:
1. Amoxicillin capsules (if child can swallow)
2. Amoxicillin chewable tablets (if age-appropriate)
3. Cefdinir suspension 125mg/5mL (for appropriate indications)
4. Azithromycin (Z-pack) for respiratory infections
Notes: Do not substitute cephalosporins in PCN allergy without assessment

AZITHROMYCIN (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 250mg tablets intermittently limited
Alternatives:
1. Azithromycin suspension (convert dose)
2. Clarithromycin 500mg BID x 7 days (for appropriate indications)
3. Doxycycline (if patient ≥8 years)

CIPROFLOXACIN (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

============================================================

PAIN/INFLAMMATION
-----------------

IBUPROFEN SUSPENSION (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Alternatives:
1. Ibuprofen chewable tablets (if age-appropriate)
2. Acetaminophen suspension (for fever/pain without inflammation)
3. Naproxen suspension (if available)

ACETAMINOPHEN IV (Status: Level 3 - Orange)
Alternatives:
1. Oral/rectal acetaminophen when appropriate
2. IV ketorolac (short-term, monitor renal function)
3. Multimodal pain management approach

NAPROXEN (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

MENTAL HEALTH
-------------

SERTRALINE (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

FLUOXETINE (Status: Level 1 - Green)
Currently available - no alternatives needed

ALPRAZOLAM (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 0.5mg tablets limited
Alternatives:
1. Use available strengths (0.25mg, 1mg)
2. Lorazepam equivalent dosing (alprazolam 0.5mg ≈ lorazepam 1mg)
3. Long-acting alternatives for chronic anxiety:
   - Clonazepam (longer half-life)
   - SSRI initiation with temporary benzodiazepine bridge
Notes: Monitor for withdrawal if changing benzodiazepines

GABAPENTIN (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 300mg capsules limited
Alternatives:
1. Use 100mg capsules (3 capsules = 300mg)
2. 400mg capsules available
3. Gabapentin solution 250mg/5mL
4. Pregabalin (Lyrica) - requires prior auth, different dosing

RESPIRATORY
-----------

ALBUTEROL MDI (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: Generic MDIs limited
Alternatives:
1. Brand Ventolin/ProAir/Proventil (check formulary tier)
2. Albuterol nebulizer solution (for patients with nebulizer)
3. Levalbuterol (Xopenex) - check formulary status

PREDNISONE TABLETS (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: 10mg and 20mg intermittently limited
Alternatives:
1. Use available strengths to achieve total dose
2. Prednisolone liquid 15mg/5mL
3. Methylprednisolone (adjust for potency: 4mg = prednisone 5mg)
4. Dexamethasone (adjust for potency: 0.75mg = prednisone 5mg)

============================================================

THYROID
-------

LEVOTHYROXINE (Status: Level 2 - Yellow)
Shortage details: Multiple strengths intermittently unavailable
Alternatives:
1. Combine available strengths to achieve dose
2. Brand Synthroid/Levoxyl (if generic unavailable)
3. DO NOT substitute between brands without TSH monitoring
Notes: Levothyroxine is narrow therapeutic index; brand changes require TSH recheck in 6-8 weeks

============================================================

ORDERING ALTERNATIVES
---------------------

When ordering shortage medications:
1. Check this list first
2. Contact pharmacy if alternative unclear
3. Document shortage-related substitution in chart
4. Notify patient of medication change
5. Schedule appropriate follow-up if switching medication class

REPORTING
---------

Report critical shortages affecting patient care to:
- Pharmacy Supply Chain: supplychain@saferx.internal
- Safety Reporting: safety@saferx.internal
